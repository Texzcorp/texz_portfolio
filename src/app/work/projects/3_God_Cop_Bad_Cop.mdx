## Overview

God Cop Bad Cop is a game where players must navigate the moral gray areas of law enforcement. In a world full of chaos, the player must switch between the roles of a "good cop" and a "bad cop", making decisions that affect the game's outcomes. The dual perspectives add complexity to the gameplay, requiring players to balance their choices carefully.

## Key Features

- **Moral Dilemmas**: The game presents players with difficult choices that test their moral compass.
- **Dual Roles**: Players switch between the "good cop" and "bad cop", each with their own set of abilities and decision-making styles.
- **Narrative-Driven Gameplay**: Every decision has consequences, leading to multiple possible endings.
- **Unique Art Style**: The game's art direction complements its dark, chaotic themes.

## Technologies Used

- **Unreal Engine 4**: For building the core mechanics and narrative structure.
- **Choice-Based System**: Custom decision-making system that tracks player actions and outcomes.

## Challenges and Learnings

Balancing the moral choices to ensure that neither the "good cop" nor the "bad cop" has an advantage required careful attention. The narrative system also needed to be tightly integrated with the gameplay to ensure that player choices felt meaningful.

## Outcome

God Cop Bad Cop offers players an immersive, thought-provoking experience that explores the complexities of moral decision-making in law enforcement. The game's branching narrative and dual-role gameplay provide a unique and engaging challenge for players.
